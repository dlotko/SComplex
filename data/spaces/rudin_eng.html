Rudin's 3-ball

Description
Rudin showed in her 1958 paper that we can triangulate a 3-tetrahedron by introducing 10 vertices on the boundary in such a way that the resulting triangulation is not shellable. This triangulation has 14 vertices and 41 facets. An interesting fact is that all the vertices can be perturbed into a position that the 3-ball is strictly convex, that is, this achieve a nonshellable 3-ball with a convex realization.
Properties
Chillingworth(1967)'s theorem shows this triangulation is simplicially collapsible. Also this example is known to be constructible.
